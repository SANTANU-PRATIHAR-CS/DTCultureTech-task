## Scoring Framework Used

| Criterion | Weight | What Was Assessed |
|-----------|--------|-------------------|
| C3 Differentiated | 20 | Patents, DSIR, USFDA, "first/largest in India" claims, specialized equipment |
| C4 Decision-Maker Quality | 15 | Founder education (IIT/PhD/NIT), ERP investment, structured operations |
| C5 Growing Sector | 15 | PLI eligibility, China+1, export tailwinds, government support |
| C6 Growth Signals | 15 | Hiring, facility expansion, certifications, website activity, financials |
| C7 Systems Maturity | 20 | SAP/ERP evidence, ISO/GMP certification depth, IT roles on LinkedIn |
| C8 Leadership Succession | 15 | Gen-2 on board, professional CFO/COO hired, independent directors |

**Bands:**
- A (80–100): Strong Federer — high priority
- B (60–79): Probable Federer — good target
- C (40–59): Borderline — research further
- D (<40): Not ICP — documented in fail log

## Key Finding

Pune's MIDC Kurkumbh (75 km from city, Daund taluka) is a dedicated red-category chemical zone with 50+ pharma and specialty chemical companies — the highest-density Federer-compatible cluster in the city. The most promising targets are companies that expanded capacity in the last 3 years, obtained new regulatory approvals (USFDA/WHO-GMP), or launched new product categories.

The single biggest scoring gap across Kurkumbh companies is **C7 (Systems Maturity)** — many capable chemistry companies run on paper-based processes with no visible ERP, which is a structural disqualifier for DeepThought's model.
